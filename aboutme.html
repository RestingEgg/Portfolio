Why Engineering?

I did not enjoy rote learning, and Engineering was an option for me to avoid that. The usual case is parents want their child to either become an engineer or a doctor in India, but I was asked to become a charted accountant, but when I explained them why engineering was good for me, they agreed.
I chose CSE in particular because I am good at Mathematics and patterns, so the field was an obvious choice for me and CSE offers innumerable roads to the future of technological innovation, which fills me with inquisitiveness and enthusiasm.


What next?

Putting things into perspective, I feel that whatever VIT has offered me was only 10% of the learning and development, and the majority lies in applying the knowledge gained, in an organisation which is dedicated to software engineering.
So I wish to work for a few years, helping me realise my strengths and interests, based upon which I will either pursue MS or an MBA. I don't have much plans to leave my country and settle abroad. Don't get me wrong with being less motivated.
